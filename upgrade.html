Almanac Dictionary

Almanac: A comprehensive catalog of diatonic voice-leading possibilities for guitar, authored by Mick Goodrick

Close Position: Notes packed within a single octave range.

Common Tone: A note shared between chords; ideally kept on the same string.

Cycle 2: Movement to the next scale degree (seconds). E.g., from C to Dm.

Cycle 3: Movement by leaps of thirds in the scale. E.g., from C to Em.

Cycle 4: Movement by leaps of fourths (the most common). E.g., from C to F.

Cycle 5: Movement by leaps of fifths. E.g., from C to G.

Cycle 6: Movement by leaps of sixths (or thirds down). E.g., from C to Am.

Cycle 7: Movement by leaps of sevenths (or a step down). E.g., from C to Bdim.

Diatonic: Using only notes belonging to the chosen scale.

Drop 2 / 3: Dropping the 2nd or 3rd voice from the top by an octave.

Guide Tones: The 3rd and 7th degrees that define a chord's character.

Spread Clusters: Tight intervals spread across strings for a modern sound.

TBN (Triad/Bass): A 4-part structure: a 3-note triad over a scale bass.

TBN 1: Triad starts on the 5th degree relative to bass (e.g. G/C).

TBN 2: Triad starts on the 6th degree relative to bass (e.g. Am/C).

Voice Leading: Moving between chords with minimal motion on each string.
